ABOUT ME

I Didn't Start Healthy. I Earned It.

I wasn't always fit, confident, or comfortable in my own body. As a first-generation Indian-American, I grew up overweight, struggling with self-esteem, and constantly comparing myself to others. No matter how hard I trained or how disciplined I tried to be, my "skinny-fat" build and genetics always seemed to hold me back. I was strong, but not lean. Active, but not truly healthy. The gap between where I was and where I wanted to be—physically, mentally, and socially—stuck with me for years.

Growing Up Indian in America

Sports were my passion, but I rarely saw anyone who looked like me succeed at the highest level. Professional athletics weren't in the cards, so I chose a different path: coaching. I earned two performance degrees from Kansas State University and worked my way into elite environments—Michigan State, the University of Oregon (competing for a National Championship), and the New York Giants in the NFL. What set me apart wasn't just credentials. It was my drive to see the whole system, not just the parts, and to challenge conventional thinking long before it was mainstream.

Facing Myself

Despite all my incredible success working in elite sport, the knowledge I had accumulated, and the position I was in—I felt like a fraud. I wasn't living it. In 2019, after accepting an alumni award, I found myself alone in a hotel room, 225 pounds and 30% body fat—strong, but metabolically unhealthy. I had become something I never thought I would nor wanted to—another unhealthy Indian, adding to the statistics.

That was my turning point.

Walking the Walk

For the first time, I put myself through the same rigorous assessments and protocols I used with NFL athletes. No quick fixes, no shortcuts—just deliberate, system-based change.

I stopped following generic "best practices" and started designing a plan that fit my Indian genetics, culture, and lifestyle. That shift changed everything.

What Actually Worked

Over the next year, I focused on fundamentals:

Simple, consistent training

Daily walks

Prioritizing sleep and recovery

Tracking data, not chasing trends

Mastering my environment and mindset

I stabilized, then optimized. I lived the Blueprxnt process.

The Outcome

Now, at 40, I'm leaner, healthier, and more energetic than ever—without medication, TRT, or extreme biohacks. My health markers rival men half my age.

More importantly, I'm calm, resilient, and present for my family and clients.

Why I Built Blueprxnt

I had no mentors for my health—especially as an Indian. I learned everything the hard way. Blueprxnt exists so others don't have to.

It's the system I wish I had at 15: one that respects genetics, culture, reality, and ambition. It's about building capacity, not burnout.

Elite performance thinking shouldn't be reserved for elite sport—it belongs to anyone ready to operate at a high level in real life.
